<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>There is a showdown brewing in Laguna Beach between boardriders,</strong> the city council, homeowners, and environmental groups over the right to manually breach the sand berm at Aliso Creek to create a standing river wave.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For decades, surfers, skimboarders, and bodyboarders have flocked to Aliso to ride its world-renowned shorebreak and, occasionally, a standing wave that forms when the creek flows into the ocean. The environmental groups, namely </span><a href="https://lagunabluebelt.org/about/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Laguna Bluebelt Coalition</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and Surfrider Foundation, say that manually breaching the berm alters the habitat and can unnecessarily introduce bacterial pollution and toxins to the ocean. The homeowners on Aliso Beach worry that the backed-up creek water threatens their properties. The city of Laguna Beach has yet to formalize a firm stance, but they do have plans to install a camera at the beach (</span><a href="https://www.ocregister.com/2023/11/15/aliso-beach-in-south-laguna-to-get-new-camera-for-increased-enforcement/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="font-weight: 400;">at a cost of $35,000</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">) to monitor, and possibly, thwart those who try to dig out the berm. Each side of the conflict is armed with differing opinions, documents, letters, and studies to support their cases.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Aliso Creek is a waterway that runs 19 miles through southern Orange County. What makes the creek well-known in the surfing world is the powerful standing wave it creates when it penetrates the berm and flows down the beachâs steep slope to the ocean. The wave occurs year round, as many as several times per month, and can last anywhere from a fleeting moment to 45 minutes. Famous vlogger </span><a href="https://www.theinertia.com/surf/jamie-obrien-just-surfed-a-massive-river-wave-that-wasnt-waimea/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Jamie OâBrien</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and Laguna Beach skim icon </span><a href="https://www.theinertia.com/surf/watch-this-big-glassy-river-wave-form/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Blair Conklin </span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">have made scores of edits featuring the wave. The creekâs watershed has been extremely urbanized over the years. It houses 160,000 people across seven cities, features a creek-side golf course less than a half-mile from its estuary, and is crossed by Interstate 5 to the east, one of the busiest thoroughfares in North America.</span></p><div class="inertia-ad-300x270 text-center center-block"><div data-type="scroll-track-ad" data-ad="300x250_Inertia_Default" pos="content-1"></div></div>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The topic of Aliso Creekâs berm is a decades-old debate that was thrust into the spotlight again last week when a city park ranger issued a $100 citation to Laguna Beach surf and skimboard videographer Greg Viviani who was starting to dig out the berm. Viviani, who goes by @solaglocal on social media, posted </span><a href="https://www.tiktok.com/@solaglocal/video/7308057637927701806" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="font-weight: 400;">a video</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> of the encounter with the park ranger on his TikTok that has been viewed more than a million times. The problem with the ticket, Viviani says, is that there is no specific city law that prevents the public from breaching the creek. The city lifeguards have also permitted boardriders to dig out the berm at times, as was the case during the most recent breach on December 7, Vivani says. The ticket that Viviani received was not for attempting to breach the berm, but for digging a hole more than two feet deep.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The opposition parties disagree, saying a law to prevent digging out the berm already exists. Surfrider Foundationâs California Policy Manager, Jennifer Savage, wrote a </span><a href="https://lagunabeachcity.granicus.com/MetaViewer.php?view_id=3&amp;event_id=2373&amp;meta_id=157224#:~:text=Commission%20staff%20have%20reviewed%20the,we%20provide%20some%20input%20with" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="font-weight: 400;">letter (page five in this PDF)</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to the county highlighting their ordinance, </span><a href="https://library.municode.com/ca/orange_county/codes/code_of_ordinances?nodeId=TIT3PUMOSAWE_DIV8PRPR_ART3LA_S3-8-25CRCACHRI" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="font-weight: 400;">3-8-25</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, which concerns creeks, canals, channels, and rivers. It declares that it is âunlawfulâ¦ to dig, move or remove any sand, gravel, rocks, dirt, soil, debrisâ¦â</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">One of the most vocal opponents of the berm breaching is Mike Beanan, a Laguna Beach resident and co-founder of the Laguna Bluebelt Coalition. He has a list of reasons why he doesnât support the boardridersâ actions. Beanan points out that the California Coastal Commission </span><a href="https://documents.coastal.ca.gov/assets/water-quality/ccc-factsheets/South-Coast/CCA%2073%20Aliso%20Creek%20Factsheet%2012-17-19.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="font-weight: 400;">identified</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Aliso Creek as a âCritical Coastal Area,â where an impaired body of water runs into a âMarine Managed Area,â in this case, Laguna Beach State Marine Reserve. Finally, Beanan cites the sand berm as essential for </span><a href="https://www.lagunaoceanfoundation.org/aliso-creek-estuary-restoration/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="font-weight: 400;">an ongoing project</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to restore the Aliso Creek estuary as a habitat for wildlife.</span></p><div class="inertia-ad-300x270 text-center center-block"><div data-type="scroll-track-ad" data-ad="300x250_Inertia_Default" pos="content-2"></div></div>
<div id="attachment_273363" style="width: 680px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-273363" decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="size-medium wp-image-273363" src="https://www.theinertia.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/glassy-670x388.jpg?x48558" alt="" width="670" height="388" srcset="https://www.theinertia.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/glassy-670x388.jpg 670w, https://www.theinertia.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/glassy-768x445.jpg 768w, https://www.theinertia.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/glassy.jpg 900w" sizes="(max-width: 670px) 100vw, 670px"><p id="caption-attachment-273363" class="wp-caption-text">Conditions behind any standing wave as a river enters the ocean can beâ¦interesting. <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">â(Laguna Beach) is a small town,â said Beanan. âEveryone knows everyone and no one, including me, wants to walk on anyone. But I swim in the ocean and I donât want to swim in urban runoff. Sea life doesnât need urban runoff.â</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The boardriders who ride the standing wave take exception to being labeled polluters. As a result, Viviani took it upon himself to represent their interests. Aside from local boardriders enjoying the wave, which he says has historical significance and legacy in Laguna Beach, the birthplace and epicenter of skimboarding, Viviani cites other reasons why manually inducing the flow of the creek is necessary. They include protecting the adjacent homes on the beach from erosion, reducing bacteria that grows in stagnant water, removing a habitat conducive for the propagation of mosquitos, and draining a pedestrian underpass that floods when the water levels get too high in the creek. Viviani has created </span><a href="https://www.change.org/p/stop-useless-laws-about-aliso-creek" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="font-weight: 400;">an online petition</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> that has garnered more than 4,000 signatures.</span></p><div class="inertia-ad-multi text-center center-block"><div data-ad="Multi_Inertia_Content" pos="content-3"></div></div>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">âThe other party says they want a natural break (of the creek) to occur,â said Viviani. âThereâs nothing natural about this anymore. You have homes that have been built, you have a bridge with a small opening that constricts the flow of the creek, you have riprap, which is the (piles of) rocks just south of the creek, and also the parking lot. Itâs not going to be a natural thing anymore. You are going to have to maintain it and correct the flow to mitigate other problems from arising.â</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The berm breaching has not only been done by surfers, skimboarders, and boogie boarders. County, and more recently, city workers have periodically opened the sand berm over the years, and did so as recently as November 18. Viviani depicts this in </span><a href="https://www.tiktok.com/@solaglocal/video/7302817339630800171" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="font-weight: 400;">another viral video</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> on his Tiktok that has received nearly ten million views.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The exasperation experienced by all sides of the issue is reflective of the </span><a href="https://www.lagunabeachindy.com/orange-county-shrugs-off-coastal-commission-cures-for-aliso-creek-breaches/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="font-weight: 400;">jurisdictional headache</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> that Aliso Beach has been subject to. Up until March of 2023, the beach was under Orange Countyâs jurisdiction and managed by OC Parks who would periodically open the berm.&nbsp; Aliso lifeguards had, at times, prevented the public from digging out the berm, while other times, they permitted it. In 2022 the California Coastal Commission </span><a href="https://lagunabeachcity.granicus.com/MetaViewer.php?view_id=3&amp;event_id=2373&amp;meta_id=157224#:~:text=Commission%20staff%20have%20reviewed%20the,we%20provide%20some%20input%20with" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span style="font-weight: 400;">called for the County</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to do more about enforcing laws regarding breaching the sand berm, demands which appear to not have had much practical impact. <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In the past few years, perhaps no one has surfed the standing river wave more than Laguna Beach professional surfer/skimboarder Blair Conklin. <div id="attachment_273364" style="width: 680px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-273364" decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="size-medium wp-image-273364" src="https://www.theinertia.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/jamiealiso-670x388.jpg?x48558" alt="" width="670" height="388" srcset="https://www.theinertia.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/jamiealiso-670x388.jpg 670w, https://www.theinertia.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/jamiealiso-1024x593.jpg 1024w, https://www.theinertia.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/jamiealiso-768x445.jpg 768w, https://www.theinertia.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/jamiealiso-900x521.jpg 900w, https://www.theinertia.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/jamiealiso.jpg 1200w" sizes="(max-width: 670px) 100vw, 670px"><p id="caption-attachment-273364" class="wp-caption-text">Jamie OâBrien, with Aliso Creek at its fullest. <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">â(The opposition) is concerned about pollutants entering the ocean, which is a valid concern that we all have in common,â said Conklin. âThe great news is that thereâs been a lot of work upstream that has led to less pollutants in the creek flow and the ocean, so itâs actually cleaner than it has ever been.
