    var scrollUpAmount = 0;

    // Add scroll event listener
    window.addEventListener('scroll', function() {
      // Get current scroll position
      const currentScrollPosition = window.pageYOffset || document.documentElement.scrollTop;
      // Check if scrolling down and past the threshold
      if (currentScrollPosition > scrollPosition && currentScrollPosition > scrollThreshold) {
        // User is scrolling down and past the threshold, add "is-toggled" class to hide the header
        header.classList.add('is-toggled');
        scrollUpAmount = 0;

        // Hide tooltip if it's visible
        const tooltip = document.getElementById('home-tooltip');
        if (tooltip && !localStorage.getItem('hideTooltip') && (tooltip.style.display === 'block' || tooltip.style.display === '')) {
          tooltip.style.display = 'none';
        }
      } else {
        // User is scrolling up or within the threshold, remove "is-toggled" class to show the header
        scrollUpAmount += scrollPosition - currentScrollPosition;
        if (scrollUpAmount > scrollThreshold) {
          header.classList.remove('is-toggled');
          scrollUpAmount = 0;

          // Show tooltip if it's hidden
          const tooltip = document.getElementById('home-tooltip');
          if (tooltip && tooltip.style.display === 'none' && !localStorage.getItem('hideTooltip')) {
            tooltip.style.display = 'block';
          }
        }
      }

      // Update scroll position
      scrollPosition = currentScrollPosition;

          const availableSpace = nav.offsetWidth - moreLink.offsetWidth - 10; // Buffer of 10px
          let totalWidth = 0;

          initialMenuItems.forEach(item => menuList.appendChild(item));
          hiddenLinks.innerHTML = '';
          hiddenLinks.classList.add('u-hide');
          moreMenu.classList.remove('is-active');

      <p>A few weeks ago, Emily Fisher was riding in a brand-new Defender 130 with a bunch of teenagers when she decided to run a quick poll. The question was simple: if it werent for getting a ride in this vehicle, would you be here? The kids thought for a minute. Then, one by one, they shook their heads.</p>
<p>Fisher is the executive director of <a href="https://ysausa.org/" rel="sponsored" data-afl-p="1" target="_blank">Youth Sports Alliance</a> (YSA), a nonprofit that helps get kids outside and improves access to sports programming in northern Utah. The teens she was with were participants of the learn-to-sail program YSA runs each spring. They came from a variety of backgrounds, ranging from longtime locals to recent immigrants from Venezuela. Several of the group were there on scholarship. If YSA hadnt been able to give them a ride, they said, theyd probably be at home, scrolling on their phones; they had no other way to get to the lake.</p>
<figure id="attachment_2672661" class="pom-image-wrap photo-alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2672661" src="https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_04939-scaled.jpg?width=730" alt="Youth Sports Alliance (YSA), a nonprofit that helps get kids outside and improves access to sports programming in northern Utah." width="2560" height="1707" srcset="https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_04939-scaled.jpg?width=300 300w, https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_04939-scaled.jpg?width=768 768w"><figcaption class="pom-caption "><span class="article__caption">Youth Sports Alliance is a nonprofit that helps get kids outside and improves access to sports programming in northern Utah.</span> (Photo: Morahan Visuals)</figcaption></figure>
<p>These are students who would never have this opportunity if we couldnt provide transportation, Fisher says. In fact, watersports of pretty much any kind would have remained out of reach.</p>
<p>Thats why YSA exists: to make sure local kids of all backgrounds can experience the wonder and empowerment of outdoor sports, from mountain biking and sailing to skiing and snowboarding. Much of YSAs work involves awarding scholarships, lending gear, and shuttling kids to and from trailheads, put-ins, and ski destinations. This last piecethe transportationhas historically been the trickiest to provide. Which is where that customized new Defender comes in.</p>

<h2>The Grand Prize</h2>
<p>When Fisher first learned about the <a href="https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-gear/cars-trucks/meet-the-winners-of-the-2023-defender-service-awards/" rel="sponsored" data-afl-p="1">Defender Service Awards</a> Presented by CHASE, she knew YSA had to apply. The competition is designed to support hardworking nonprofits across the United States and Canada. This year, it included an award category specifically for Outdoor Accessibility and Education. Fisher was sure YSA would be a perfect fit.</p>
<p>But the competition was fierce and the stakes were high. After a first round of internal selection and a second round of community voting, six winners would ultimately be awarded a total of $32,500 and a brand-new customized Defender 130. The money would be nice, of course, but Fisher had her eyes on that car.</p>
<figure id="attachment_2672662" class="pom-image-wrap photo-alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2672662" src="https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_00622-scaled.jpg?width=730" alt="" width="2560" height="1708" srcset="https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_00622-scaled.jpg?width=300 300w, https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_00622-scaled.jpg?width=768 768w"><figcaption class="pom-caption "><span class="article__caption">The six winners of the 2023 awards received a total of $32,500 and a brand-new customized Defender 130.</span> (Photo: Morahan Visuals)</figcaption></figure>
<p>At this point, YSA didnt have a single vehicle to call its ownpretty wild, considering that such a huge part of the nonprofits mission involves providing transportation.</p>
<p>Almost every week, were picking up kids from four to five different schools and bringing them to sports programming across the Wasatch Range, Fisher says. During the 20232024 school year alone, the nonprofit had to rent 234 vehicles. Each one cost $400 on average. Which meant <i>a lot </i>of the organizations limited funds were going to transportation costs.</p>
<p>So, when the 2023 Defender Service Awards came along, Fisher and her team dropped everything to apply. It paid off: after weeks spent creating a video, galvanizing community support, and biting their nails during the intense public voting period, they <a href="https://www.outsideonline.com/outdoor-adventure/snow-sports/youth-sports-alliance-defender-awards/" rel="sponsored" data-afl-p="1">won the grand prize</a>. Last March, the check hit their bank account and the Defender landed on their doorstep.</p>
<figure id="attachment_2672664" class="pom-image-wrap photo-alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2672664" src="https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_05372-scaled.jpg?width=730" alt="Youth Sports Alliance" width="2560" height="1708" srcset="https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_05372-scaled.jpg?width=300 300w, https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_05372-scaled.jpg?width=768 768w"><figcaption class="pom-caption "><span class="article__caption">Youth Sports Alliance touches the lives of more than 2,200 youth each year.</span> (Photo: Morahan Visuals)</figcaption></figure>
<p>I still cant believe we won it, Fisher says. It doesnt feel real.</p>
<h2>A Dream Come True</h2>
<p>Every time Fisher steps into her office parking lot these days, she has to pinch herself. The new Defender 130 is custom wrapped, featuring YSAs bold logo. The roof rack is fitted for skis, and the back tailgate sports a huge bike rack. The vehicle has eight seats, maximizing efficiency on each trip. The whole thing is rugged, kitted out, state of the art. Its meticulously customized for YSAs mission. Its everything Fisher could have hoped for.</p>
<p>While the YSA team did host a few events to celebrate the vehicles arrivalincluding a party at Deer Valley Ski Resort that was attended by Lindsey Vonnthey mostly just focused on getting the vehicle integrated into their operation. They shook some hands, thanked their community, brought the car home, and got right to work.</p>
<figure id="attachment_2672665" class="pom-image-wrap photo-alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2672665" src="https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_02543-scaled.jpg?width=730" alt="Youth Sports Alliance" width="2560" height="1708" srcset="https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_02543-scaled.jpg?width=300 300w, https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_02543-scaled.jpg?width=768 768w"><figcaption class="pom-caption "><span class="article__caption">Winning the 2023 Defender Service Awards Outdoor Accessibility and Education category was a dream come true for Youth Sports Alliance.</span> (Photo: Morahan Visuals)</figcaption></figure>
<h2>The Hardest-Working Car in the Wasatch</h2>
<p>Some days, YSA uses the Defender to cover three programs at once. The roomy cargo space easily fits heaps of life jackets and other gear for spring sailing courses, and the tailgate rack accommodates up to six bikes. On busy weekends, YSA uses the Defender to make up to 14 trips per day. Few SUVs work harder.</p>
<p>Having the car has already allowed YSA to dramatically cut back on its vehicle rentals. And since every dollar the nonprofit saves goes directly back into funding scholarships, that means tons more kids will be able to participate in programs this fall and winter.</p>
<figure id="attachment_2672666" class="pom-image-wrap photo-alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2672666" src="https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_03072-scaled.jpg?width=730" alt="Youth Sports Alliance" width="2560" height="1707" srcset="https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_03072-scaled.jpg?width=300 300w, https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_03072-scaled.jpg?width=768 768w"><figcaption class="pom-caption "><span class="article__caption">Youth Sports Alliance uses their Defender 130 to make up to 14 trips per day.</span> (Photo: Morahan Visuals)</figcaption></figure>
<p>Right now, over the course of a four-week program, were saving $1,200 [by using the Defender in lieu of a rental], Fisher says. It costs YSA around $200 to fund a kids winter ski program, so that $1,200 in savings means six more kids will be able to ski on scholarship this year. The Defender is, quite literally, a gift that keeps on giving.</p>
<p>This winter, the vehicle will likely pay even greater dividends. Capable, reliable, and equipped with four-wheel drive, the car handles snowy mountain roads effortlessly.</p>
<p>Its funnyitll be sunny all week, but it always seems to be super snowy every weekend we have to shuttle kids to our programs. Its definitely winter driving up there, Fisher says. This car is really built for our environment, and were excited to have it to transport these kids safely.</p>
<figure id="attachment_2672667" class="pom-image-wrap photo-alignnone"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2672667" src="https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_07836-scaled.jpg?width=730" alt="Youth Sports Alliance" width="2560" height="1707" srcset="https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_07836-scaled.jpg?width=300 300w, https://cdn.outsideonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/morahan_visuals_outside_tv_land_rover_ysa_07836-scaled.jpg?width=768 768w"><figcaption class="pom-caption "><span class="article__caption">Capable, reliable, and equipped with four-wheel drive, the Defender 130 handles mountain roads effortlessly.</span> (Photo: Morahan Visuals)</figcaption></figure>
<p>A touch of amazement still lingers in Fishers voice when she says this, as if it all still seems too good to be true.</p>
<p>No one gets into nonprofit work to make money. You dont expect a lot of supportyou just do it because youre passionate about it. Fisher says. But then something like this happens. And its just been incredible.</p>
